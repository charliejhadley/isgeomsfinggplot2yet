Is geom_sf in ggplot2 yet?


NO.

The version of ggplot2 on CRAN does not yet have support for visualising sf objects.

Until it does, you could use the dev version: 

devtools::install_github("tidyverse/ggplot2")
Wait... sf?

sf is a fairly young (first commit in 2016) package for working doing GIS stuff in R.

It's incredible. You're highly recommend to migrate from sp and/or rgdal

Here are five excellent reasons to use sf:

  • sf can easily convert to/from sp and other popular GIS ad R data formats.
  • sfsimplifies importing GIS datasets compared to both sp and rgdal
  • sf objects are compatible with a tidyverse (dplyr, tidyr etc) workflow
  • sf has built in tools for geomtric calculations (union, intersect, area, great circles, etc)
  • sf development was initiated with (and continues to receive) R Consortium funding and favour.

Read about sf here https://r-spatial.github.io/sf/
